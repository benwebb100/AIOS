# Brand — AutoOS

## Name
**AutoOS.** The product we install is the AIOS (AI Operating System); the agency that installs it is AutoOS. Use "AutoOS" for the company, "AIOS" / "AI operating system" for the thing.

## Positioning
We install AI operating systems for business owners. Not chatbots, not Zapier flows — a persistent AI brain that knows the whole business and runs it with the owner.

**Avoid:** "AI automation agency" (commoditised, vague, lumped in with Zapier/Make point solutions).

**Use:** *"We set up an AI operating system that actually knows your business — your team, your clients, your numbers, your processes — and runs it with you."*

## Differentiator
Other people sell point solutions (a chatbot, an automation, an agent). We install the substrate underneath them — the contextualised Claude Code workspace + Obsidian knowledge base + MCP integrations — so the system **compounds** in value the longer the client uses it. The longer they stay, the more it knows, the harder it is to leave. That's the moat.

## Voice
- **Direct, plain, no jargon.** Say "we set this up for you," not "we deliver bespoke AI transformation."
- **Confident, not hypey.** Cite specific outcomes (15–20 hrs/week saved, 60–70% of recurring tasks automated).
- **Operator to operator.** The audience runs businesses; talk like a peer who's solved their problem, not like a vendor.

## One-Liner
*"You stop using ChatGPT like a search engine and start using AI as a system that actually knows and runs your business."*

## Target Customer (Quick Reference)
- Business owners / operators, **$200K–$5M revenue**
- Already use ChatGPT/Claude (early-adopter mindset)
- Knowledge-heavy or service-based business
- They are the bottleneck in their own business
- Age 28–55, AU initially, expanding to UK/US

## Visual Identity

**Direction:** Developer-tools / infrastructure aesthetic. Lineage: Linear, Vercel, Cursor, Railway, Notion. Operator-grade, type-driven, monochromatic with one signal accent. Deliberately *not* "AI agency" (no purple/blue gradients, no robot/brain iconography, no stock photos of diverse teams in glass offices).

### Logo
Wordmark only: `autoos`, all lowercase, JetBrains Mono Bold. Optional small amber filled square `▮` before the wordmark — reads as cursor / status indicator. The square doubles as the favicon and ad-grid avatar.

### Palette
| Role | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | `#0A0F0E` | Warm near-black. Type and primary surfaces. |
| Background | `#F5F2ED` | Warm off-white (paper, not stark). Default canvas. |
| Accent | `#FF6B1A` | Signal amber. One element per slide / page max. |
| Muted | `#A8A29A` | Soft grey. Secondary type, dividers. |

### Typography
- **Display/headlines:** Inter (Söhne if budget allows). Geometric sans, tight tracking, big weight contrast.
- **Body:** Inter Regular.
- **Accent (numbers, stats, status labels):** JetBrains Mono or Space Mono.

### Layout principles
- Generous white space — every slide / section breathes.
- One idea per surface.
- Numbers and stats are huge (60–100pt monospace).
- Grid-based, deliberate, almost editorial.
- No drop shadows, no glassmorphism, no gradients, no AI clichés.
