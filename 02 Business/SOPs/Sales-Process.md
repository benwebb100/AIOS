# SOP — Sales Process (audit-first)

> Conversation → paid audit → presentation → architecture sale → setup → retainer. End-to-end flow for closing a client.
> Source: Master Playbook Part 3 + Bledsoe webinar synthesis (April 2026).

## 1. Lead

- Source: warm outreach (Phase 1), event/coworking intro, referral, or paid funnel (Phase 2+)
- Add to `Clients.md` Prospects table at stage `lead`

## 2. Initial Conversation

See `Discovery-Call.md`. Goal: understand the business well enough to offer the audit. **Do not pitch the AIOS.** Listen, ask questions, leave with permission to send a paid-audit proposal.

## 3. Paid Audit

See `Paid-Audit.md`. Founding price $250–500. Delivers a "cookbook" document and a live demo. The audit *is* the close — by presentation day, the architecture sale is a formality.

## 4. Architecture Proposal (post-audit)

Populate this template using the cookbook produced by the audit:

```
PROPOSAL FOR: [Business Name]
DATE: [Date]
PREPARED BY: Bailey — AutoOS

WHAT THE AUDIT FOUND
- [Top 3 opportunities surfaced in the cookbook]

WHAT I'D BUILD

Executive side (you, personally):
- [Owner-facing automations from cookbook]

Operations side (your business):
- [Company-side automations from cookbook]

INVESTMENT
- Architecture setup: $[X] one-time
- Monthly retainer: $[X]/month
- Modular add-ons: $[X] each (toggle on/off)
- Founding pricing locked for 6 months (if applicable)

NEXT STEPS
1. Sign off
2. Share tool logins / API keys (checklist sent)
3. Schedule setup session (2–4 hrs)
4. AIOS live within 48 hours
```

## 5. Pricing Conversation — Anchoring

Anchor the architecture price against four value levers (roll into a number, charge 10–20% of the total):

1. **Cash-on-cash** — what will this save or earn them in the next 12 months?
2. **Time value** — hours saved × their effective hourly rate.
3. **Pain avoidance** — problems and inconsistencies eliminated (mistakes, missed follow-ups, late invoices).
4. **Future opportunities** — what does this make possible? (compounding upside, infinite ceiling)

If the rolled-up value is $50K, charge $5–10K. If it's $200K, charge $20–40K. Never quote a number until you can justify it against these four.

## 6. Price as a Filter

If they baulk at the audit price ($250–500), they will baulk at the architecture price ($2K–5K) and they will baulk at the retainer. They are risk-averse and price-sensitive — not the right client. **Use price as a qualifying filter**, not as a sticking point to negotiate down.

You want clients who treat AI as a growth investment, not a cost to minimise.

## 7. Close

- Send architecture proposal within 24 hrs of presentation
- Calendar follow-up 24 hrs later if no reply
- Common objections + scripts: see Master Playbook Appendix C

## 8. Setup Session

- 2–4 hrs, remote (or in-person for premium tier)
- Install Layers 0–2 (foundation, context, data) + first automation
- Full step-by-step: Master Playbook Part 3 / Phase 2
- Update `Clients.md` — move from `proposal sent` → `closed-won`, then to **Active Clients** table

## 9. First Week

- Daily check-ins on the live automation (typically the daily brief)
- Day 7: 15-min check-in call, capture feedback
- Refine context, fix integrations

## 10. Retainer Kicks In

- 1–2 automations / month
- Context updates and maintenance
- Monthly 30-min strategy call
- Async support (24-hr response on business days)

## Scope Discipline

**In retainer:** 1–2 automations/month, context updates, monthly call, async support.

**NOT in retainer:** Custom software builds, marketing execution, 24/7 on-call, anything > 8–10 hrs/month per client.

If the ask is bigger, scope it as a separate project with a separate fee. Never let it creep.
