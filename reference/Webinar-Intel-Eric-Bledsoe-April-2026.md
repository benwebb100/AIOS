### Key Quote

## The Audit-First Model

Eric doesn't sell AIOS directly. He sells a **paid discovery audit.**

### Process
1. **Lunch** — meet the business owner, listen to their problems
2. **Proposal** — send SOW for an audit (paid engagement)
3. **Stakeholder interviews** — 90 min each, recorded (phone recorder, Granola, or Plaud)
   - Start with ownership group (~3 hours)
   - Individual owner interviews
   - Key employee/stakeholder interviews
   - Pricing: **$1,215–$1,500 per conversation**
   - Max 3 meetings/day, processes in the afternoon
4. **Analysis** — runs transcripts through AI, creates "cookbook" document
   - Top section: plain English that anyone can understand
   - Bottom section: technical data (doubles as project requirements doc)
   - Maps current state → future state → what to automate (by risk)
5. **Presentation** — handful of slides + live demos
   - "Puppy dog close" — let them hold it, see it work
   - Leaves presentation on a URL so they can play with it after
   - "I have not had to sell it or convince anybody. It sells itself."

### Why This Is Powerful
- Gets paid to learn the client's business (funds education)
- The audit itself IS the close — by presentation day, they're already sold
- What the client originally wanted is rarely what you end up selling (you uncover bigger needs)
- De-risks for client: "You wouldn't trust a surgeon without diagnostics"

### Key Quote
> "You don't sell widgets, you sell discoveries, which unlock unlimited potentials."

## Pricing Model: Architecture + Modular Add-ons

### Structure
- **Architecture (AIOS Foundation):** $10,000 for first employee, $2,500 per additional seat
- **Modular solutions:** individual features sold as toggle-on/off add-ons
- Two separate sales:
  - **Executive side** — personal productivity for the owner/C-suite
  - **Operations side** — company-level systems (where the BIG money is)

### Value Anchoring (4 Levers)
1. **Cash-on-cash** — what will it cost vs. what will they make back this year?
2. **Time value** — hours saved × hourly rate
3. **Time returned** — freed bandwidth for growth (separate from #2)
4. **Future opportunities** — what does this make possible? (infinite upside)

Roll all 4 into a number, charge 10-20% of that as the fee. If value = $100K, fee = $10-20K.

### Revenue Breakdown (Eric's $200K)
- $124,000 from ONE company (executive + operations)
- ~$80,000 from individual/small team engagements (teams of 3-5 + solopreneurs)
- Prefers high-ticket cash upfront over monthly retainers
- Does charge ops retainer for ongoing, but cash infusion is the priority

### Key Quote
> "I would much rather have a cash infusion of $10,000 to $20,000 up front. Bills are paid for the next year."

## Delivery: Claude Interface, No Custom Dashboard

- **Builds on Claude Code** but **delivers via Claude desktop app (Cowork)** to clients
- Clients get the Claude graphical interface — NOT a custom dashboard, NOT CLI
- Physically installs on client computers (hard drive, drag and drop)
- 75% of work done during discovery/audit phase (context engineering)
- 25% is per-employee context customisation
- In-person team training via "luncheon learns" (lunch + training, 1-2 weeks)
- Also installs tools like WhisperFlow for productivity

### Why No Dashboard
- Business owners don't want CLI — they want a graphical interface
- Claude's desktop app IS the interface — no need to build one
- The value is in the context engineering, not the UI
- "Owners don't buy AIOS. They buy outputs."

### Key Quote
> "My clients don't want to work on CLI. They want a graphic interface. So I build it in Claude Code but I only teach my clients how to use it on Claude Cowork."

## Implications for AIOS Agency (Bailey)

### 1. Dashboard — CONFIRMED: Don't Build It
Eric's entire $200K business uses Claude's native interface. No custom front end. The value is context engineering and outputs, not a dashboard. This is definitive proof the council verdict was correct.

### 2. Meta Ads — Likely Wrong Channel for Launch
Eric's success is 100% in-person networking + referrals. The council flagged this as a blind spot. Bailey should consider leading with in-person/warm outreach rather than cold Meta Ads, at least until he has case studies and testimonials.

### 3. Pricing — Bailey Is Potentially Underpricing by 5-10x
Eric charges $10K for first seat vs Bailey's $1,500 setup. Even accounting for Eric's 25 years of business experience and authority, the gap is enormous. Bailey's founding pricing ($1,500 + $1K/mo) may be anchoring too low. Consider:
- The paid audit model (get paid for discovery instead of doing it free)
- Higher setup fees even at founding level
- Per-seat pricing for teams

### 4. The Audit-First Model — Major Playbook Upgrade
Bailey's current flow: free discovery call → proposal → setup. Eric's flow: paid audit ($7-9K) → presentation with live demos → architecture sale ($10K+) → modular add-ons (ongoing). The audit-first model:
- Generates revenue BEFORE the main sale
- Builds deeper understanding of the client's business
- Makes the close effortless (puppy dog close)
- Filters out bad clients via price

### 5. Cash Upfront vs Monthly Retainer
Eric explicitly prefers large upfront payments over retainers. "Thrust required to escape gravity" — cash runway matters more than recurring revenue when you're early. Bailey should consider whether the retainer model is right for launch or if high-ticket upfront is better.

### 6. In-Person Delivery Is the Trust Builder
Physical presence, handshakes, eye contact — then the AI conversation. Bailey should consider whether his launch strategy should be local-first, in-person, rather than remote-first via ads.

### 7. "Not Only the President, Also a Client"
Eric's #1 sales tool is being a genuine user of the system. Personal conviction from daily use. Bailey needs to have his own AIOS running his own business before selling it to others — Sprint 0 in the playbook.
